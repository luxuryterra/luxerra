Concept I

Dark & Dramatic

A bold residential kitchen designed for high-floor urban living. Matte charcoal cabinetry absorbs the glow of Manhattan's skyline, turning the city itself into a backdrop rather than a view. The white Calacatta island creates a luminous counterpoint — a place to gather, eat, and look out at the grid below.

Industrial pendants with warm Edison-style bulbs pull the eye toward the cooking zone while a herringbone dark oak floor grounds the palette, keeping the energy sophisticated rather than cold. Two lighting scenarios — night and day — were rendered to demonstrate how dramatically the same space shifts with available light.

Concept II

White Marble
& Warm Oak

An open-plan kitchen bathed in natural light. White resin cabinetry rises to ceiling height while oak-grain upper cabinets break the run with warmth. The marble island — clad in fluted oak on its base — doubles as a casual dining counter.

Black steel crittall frames and matte black hardware thread a quiet contrast through the otherwise all-light palette. Globe pendants in clear glass cast unobtrusive pools of light over the workspace below.
